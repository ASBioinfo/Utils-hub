In this example, pay attention to the -rtu --delete options.
 We have already discussed the -r and -t optionsâ€“recursive copy and time stamp copy. 
The -u option tells rsync to update only different/changed/added files from the SOURCE to the DESTINATION. 
The option --delete will remove any files from the DESTINATION that the SOURCE may no longer have. 
With this (and other options, like -l for symlinks) you can keep directories in multiple locations up to date with the source.
